SimStep Overview

Abstract

Interactive simulations have the potential to enhance STEM learning, yet their effectiveness is often limited by reliance on generic, off-the-shelf content that fails to meet diverse learners' needs. While generative AI can enable educators to rapidly author custom simulations, existing approaches require significant technical expertise or complex iterative prompting to align the learning objectives and simulation behavior. We present SimStep, an authoring tool leveraging multi-level abstractions to incrementally specify, validate, and debug AI-generated educational simulations. Teachers progressively specify simulation detailsâ€”moving systematically from learning concepts to specific scenarios, pedagogical goals, and interactive behaviors. To address ambiguities in AI-generated code, SimStep introduces an underspecification resolution engine coupled with visual debugging widgets, enabling teachers to interactively resolve simulation behaviors without directly manipulating code. Additionally, it proactively generates targeted test cases to guide teachers in systematically verifying and correcting simulation behavior. Evaluations with educators demonstrate that SimStep significantly reduces authoring complexity, enhances pedagogical alignment, and empowers teachers to create personalized, learner-centered simulations.
